Description:

Chimera (CMRA) is an ERC-20 token that runs privately on the Ethereum blockchain. Its main goal is to remove the transparency seen in typical Ethereum tokens, effectively eliminating transactions being logged by services such as Etherscan. This is to give token holders the advantage of staying anonymous - the wallet address of token holders cannot be seen by anyone and transactions remain unseen. The max supply of CMRA is 100 million tokens. Only the initial contract creation transaction can be seen. No token transactions or holders can easily be traced back to the token contract. To demonstrate, you can see proof of concept on our Etherscan Token Listing.

Token Logistics:

CMRA is a private token acting as an alternate form of currency/exchange. CMRA will be used for services provided in the future by the developers. Beginners guide to Chimera: What is Chimera? A currency? A security? A stake? Chimera is an ERC-20 token (Ethereum) aiming to remove the transparency seen in typical Ethereum tokens. The pros and cons for transparency vary from person to person, with some people preferring an asset such as Monero over something like Bitcoin. Chimera is essentially Ethereum's solution to creating an anonymous service on Ethereum's blockchain. Thus, Chimera can be thought of a "private version of Ethereum" - Chimera holds all the capabilities as Ethereum with reduced transparency. As the development team plans to have Chimera be used as a payment method, it can be seen as a currency. Why buy Chimera? As Chimera can be seen as a form of "private Ethereum", anyone who is interested in escaping the transparency of the blockchain to hide large amounts of funds would benefit from Chimera's technology. Using Chimera's services leaves no paper trails, thus giving benefit to those seeking sanction from taxing authorities (although our team does not indorse this). Chimera tokens can also used as forms of payment towards services our team will offer in the future. What will Chimera offer to holders/buyers? Chimera holders get the benefit of not being listed under the token contract address on modern block explorers. Transactions themselves can only be viewed in raw hexadecimal to get the values of transaction functions. This information (reguarily displayed for ERC-20 tokens) has been hidden by our token, as services such as WhaleWatcher.io periodically scan the blockchain for large transactions and announces this information publically. For large coin holders, whale watching services can cause a hurdle. Chimera and it's services aim to surpass this hurdle. What incentives does Chimera give? Chimera can be seen as a private form of Ethereum. It contains all the core functionalities of the Ethereum token, but removes the transparency associated. Using Chimera as a currency provides incentives such as secure and anonymous transfers, a store of value over time, and early access to the products and services the Chimera team will be offering. How is this different than any other ERC-20 token? Chimera differs from most ERC-20 tokens in the sense that the token contract will not display the holders or quantites. This means that making a script/tool to collect the information of token holders is much harder to achieve than regular ERC-20 tokens. If Chimera is sent through it's Scrambler service, it creates a chain of transactions effectively making the time complexity of a script of software exponential rather than linear. The only "easy to see" transactions are from the inital contract creation, minting, and burning. But wait - can't we still technically see transactions if we look hard enough? While block explorers such as Etherscan typically have trouble displaying the "To" address and amount with our token, it is not technically impossible to trace transactions. In contrast, PRIVATE token (A.K.A. Buccaneer Coin) was similar, and their lead developer saw the transparency/tracing problem. In response to this, they "Pump and dumped" their token and proceeded to exit scam. Fortunately, Chimera's goal is to solve the problem PRIVATE failed to: by centralizing transactions before redirecting them appropriately. While more users take part in Chimera's Scrambler, the difficilty of tracing the recipients grows exponentially. As more "middle wallets" are added, the complexity becomes even stronger. What's in store for the future? As of now, we have our first mainnet service working at http://chimera.exchange. This services allows you to further conceal a transfer of funds. Future projects include a crypto-based subscription website for content and media creators.
